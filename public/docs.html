What MyAITwin does

MyAITwin MCP is a personal RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) database that connects directly to Claude. It gives Claude long-term memory of everything you choose to store: your knowledge, your skills, your voice, your principles. When you ask Claude to help you create something, it searches your twin first. The output sounds like you, not like generic AI.

How it works

MyAITwin uses two layers:

  • Supabase. Stores your knowledge as structured records with types, tags, and provenance.
  • Pinecone. Stores semantic vector embeddings so you can search by meaning, not just keywords.

When you search your twin, both layers work together. Keyword and semantic search return results ranked by relevance.

The 19 tools

MyAITwin provides 19 MCP tools across four categories.

Storing knowledge

add_knowledge
Store a typed, tagged knowledge item.
add_voice_note
Store a voice note transcript with automatic extraction.
add_document
Store a long document with automatic chunking.
add_from_url
Fetch and store a web page.
add_reference_record
Store a creation event linking knowledge and skills used.

Retrieving knowledge

search_twin
Semantic search across all knowledge.
search_for_creation
Dual search returning skills and knowledge separately.
get_by_type
Retrieve all items of a specific type.
get_by_tag
Retrieve all items with a specific tag.
list_recent
List recently added items.

Understanding your twin

get_schema
Overview of what types and how many items you have.
get_sources
List all source documents.
find_patterns
Surface recurring patterns across your knowledge.
synthesise
Synthesise across multiple knowledge items on a topic.

Managing your twin

update_knowledge
Update an existing item.
delete_knowledge
Delete an item.
add_schema_type
Define a new knowledge type.
update_schema_type
Update an existing type definition.
get_welcome
Session initialisation and system prompt.

Getting started

  1. Visit myaitwin.lutolearn.com. Enter your email.
  2. Click the magic link in your email.
  3. Copy your MCP URL from the /create page.
  4. In Claude Desktop: Settings → Connectors → Add custom connector → paste your URL.
  5. Start a new conversation. Your twin is connected.

Example prompts

Three working prompts that demonstrate core MyAITwin functionality. Paste any of these into a fresh Claude Desktop conversation with your twin connected.

Example 1. Storing a principle

"Store this as a principle: I always structure presentations from general to specific. Start with the big picture, then drill into the detail. This applies to every presentation I give."

add_knowledge Shows typed storage, automatic tagging, the propose-confirm-store flow.
Example 2. Retrieving knowledge

"What do I have stored about proposal writing? Give me everything relevant."

search_twin Shows semantic retrieval, inline source and date citations, provenance tags.
Example 3. Creating from stored knowledge

"Help me write a LinkedIn post about why professionals should build their own AI knowledge layer. Use whatever I have stored that fits."

search_for_creation Shows dual retrieval, skill application, output that sounds like you.

Troubleshooting

Twin not responding. Start a fresh Claude conversation. The system prompt establishes on session start.

Retrieval returning nothing. Try different search terms. Semantic search works best with natural language queries.

Storage timing out. Large documents (over 100KB) may take 30 to 60 seconds. If it fails, split the document and try again.

Lost your MCP URL. Visit /create and use the Regenerate button. Your old URL will stop working immediately.
